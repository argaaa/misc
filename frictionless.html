Prologue · The Individual Loop

The paradox: struggle builds the comfort that unravels it.

Effort produces strength. Strength produces comfort. Comfort produces fragility. Fragility forces effort again.

  • 1 · Struggle — forces adaptation, no shortcuts.
  • 2 · Strength — capability earned through friction.
  • 3 · Comfort — the reward strength makes possible.
  • 4 · Fragility — comfort left unchecked, erodes it. Back to 1.

Prologue · The Generational Loop

Zoom out, and the pattern repeats across generations.

Hardship produces strong people. Strong people build good times. Good times raise weak people. Weak people bring hardship again.

  • Each stage isn't a single event — it's a generation's worth of drift, roughly 20–30 years.
  • Nobody chooses which stage they're born into. Only how they respond to it.
cf. Strauss–Howe generational theory
